## Individual Contributions

Each member led one labeled empirical investigation in the Phase 2 notebook, in line with the project requirement of at least one question per team member. The investigations are presented in the notebook in the order described below.

**Kavish Jain** led the disposal-delay and court-inequality investigation, examining how disposal time varies across bail types and High Courts and quantifying the gap between observed and composition-adjusted disposal time at the court level. He authored the adjusted court delay index and the percentile distribution analysis (median, p75, p90, p99) that ground the notebook's claims about systematic court-level differences.

**Pranav Pipariya** led the long-delay risk modeling investigation, examining how filing-stage metadata — bail type, court, case type, and filing timing — predicts whether a disposed case crosses the long-delay threshold, and turning that pattern into a calibrated risk benchmark validated on a temporal hold-out. He authored the lift, calibration, and top-coefficient analyses that anchor the notebook's predictive findings.

**Tanishq Gupta** led the bail-type landscape investigation, examining how regular bail, anticipatory bail, and cancellation cases are distributed across High Courts, filing years, and case-type conventions, and authored the interpretation of structural heterogeneity in bail filings. He produced the cross-court bail-mix tables and the top-case-types-by-bail breakdown that establish the registry-convention context the downstream investigations rely on.

**Tushar Singh** led the pending-burden and COVID-period investigation, examining how pending case counts and pending durations vary across courts and filing years, and interpreting the pendency surge observed in the 2020–2021 window. He authored the pending-rate summaries by court and the year-over-year filing-and-pending-rate trend analysis that frame how the snapshot of unresolved cases should be read.

**Yash Kumar** led the observed-outcome investigation, examining how disposal-outcome labels — granted, rejected, withdrawn, and other — distribute across bail types and courts within the labeled subset of the disposed cohort. He authored the outcome-mix tables and the coverage-caveats discussion that establish how restricted-sample patterns should and should not be generalized to the full bail population.
